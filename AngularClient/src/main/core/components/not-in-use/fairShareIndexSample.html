Fair Share Index - Income Inequality Part II: Sharers vs. Keepers

How to help closing the gap between rich and poor?

Most of the organizations in the market uses a classic approach; the business owner makes the initial investment while creating the business and sets the rules in favor of himself, most importantly this lets him to get all the profit. Meaning that even after years of work and significant contributions, most of the employees will not get a share from organization's income but get a fixed salary. In the long term, since the organization owners keep getting a bigger pie from the income and not sharing it accordingly, this type of income distribution becomes another root cause of 'Income Inequality'.

If we could encourage organizations to measure or at least consider all contributions, including the owner's initial investment, and then share the income to anyone in the organization, we could prevent this dynamic and fixed income difference. Such approach would have a significant impact on the society in overall, the difference between the owners and the workers would be much more small and fair.

By using Fair Share Index, we can categorize the organizations by their approach on how they distribute their income, whether they share the profit with their employees or not.

In the following example, we will use two categories under Fair Share Index;

  • Keepers: Uses the classic approach; the owner gets all the profit, workers get a fixed salary.
  • Sharers: Considers contributions from all parties and share the income based on this outcome.
Again it's up to us to decide and rate these categories. Then the organizations will get an income from the pool based on their approach.

Unlike the other indexes, Fair Share Index doesn't have a solid base as a difference between the organization. It requires a standard method to be developed; how to make a distinction between 'Sharer' and 'Keeper' organizations. When an organization declares that it is a 'Sharer', how to ensure that this is actually the case.

Also, one of the important parts to be an owner of an organization is to have various responsibilities. For example, what will happen when the organization is going to be smaller or will get bankrupt? Therefore, sharing the income should also mean sharing the responsibilities? To be able to deal with these type of details, we may need new type of contracts between organizations and workers.

Today, as a legal form, 'Non-Profit Organization' can be the closest approach that can cover Fair Share Index and can be a good starting point. Otherwise, without studying it further, this might be the least practical index within these samples. Still, we believe it is a good food for thought on how we could approach to 'Income Inequality' issue.
