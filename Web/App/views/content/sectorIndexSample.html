Sector Index: The Big Picture

Every organization in the system is trying to solve one of the society's issues; food, shelter, healthcare, clothing, education, entertainment and so on. However, their importance for the society differ.

One of the issues in the current system is that the consumers usually interact with only the organizations about their daily needs and allocate the resources based on this interaction, instead of their importance.

For example, one might think that education sector is more important for the society than most of the other sectors. On the other side, in order to make transactions with a sector, you should become a consumer of that sector, in this case either be a student or a relative of a student. If not, it would be of the routine spendings and cannot be supported in a systematic way.

We believe, if we could directly set each sectors' priorities by comparing them side by side and could distribute the resources based on this, the final picture would be quite different than the current one.

Sector Index aims to give us the opportunity to see the overall of the system and let us distribute the resources to the organizations based on the importance of their work.

To make the reasoning clear behind this new approach; if the society could solve its fundamental issues, it would be getting much easier to focus on higher level ones.
